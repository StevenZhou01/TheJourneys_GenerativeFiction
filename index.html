The immigration of European refugees from 2004 to 2032

 

 

  In April 2004, Nathaniel made the decision to begin the journey of finding a better life in another place. Nathaniel was a man about 20 years old from South Sudan. He decided to leave due to the violation of human rights in his country. Although he understood the journey might be unpredictable and dangerous, he had no choice. The journey would begin from South Sudan to Lampedusa.

  This year, the Italian and Libyan governments reached a secret agreement that obliged Libya to accept African immigrants deported from Italian territories.

  On the way to Lampedusa, Nathaniel also met some other people. Camila was a mom carrying her two kids suffering from the unemployment problem in South Sudan. Gabriel was a 12-year-old boy from Pakistan, who left as a result of natural disasters. They became partners in the journey.

  After around a month of walking, they arrived in a small town close to the border. Everyone was exhausted. Nathaniel tried to ask the direction, but they were told the border was closed, so they had to change to some other places. However, Gabriel was getting sick. Nathaniel and Camila were both worried about him, and if they could go to Europe.

  Several days later, they still couldn't find the port that could go to Europe. Gabriel's disease was becoming more and more severe. Ultimately, they got lost in a desert without food and water. Everyone was in despair and waiting for the coming of death.

 

  In June 2005, Naomi decided to start the journey of finding a better life. Naomi was a mom having a baby from Pakistan. She made this determination to leave due to racial persecution in her country. Although she knew the journey would be long and uncertain, she still had to leave the current place. The journey would begin from Pakistan to Morocco and go to Spain.

  This year, there was a rise in the number of people living in refugee-like situations within their own countries.

  On the way to Spain, Naomi also met some other people. Julia was a lady who had just gotten pregnant suffering from racial persecution in Pakistan. Arthur was a 10-year-old boy from Ethiopia, who left because of food scarcity. The long journey was no longer lonely.

  After around a month of walking, they arrived in a small town close to the border. Everyone was overtired. Naomi tried to ask the direction, but they were told the border was closed, so they had to change to some other places. However, Julia was getting indisposed. Naomi and Arthur were both worried about her, and if they could go to Europe.

  Since the original plan didn't work, they changed to head to a port in Libya. However, when they arrived in Libya, they were arrested by the Libyan coast guards in a refugee camp. There were many people in the refugee camp from different places. Waylon was a farmer from Iraq. Waylon said without any emotion, " You guys shouldn't come here. It's a hell..." After a few days, Arthur was beaten by the police. Julia also passed away since her disease was getting more and more severe but couldn't receive proper medical treatment. Fortunately, at an accidental moment, they found a chance to escape from the camp. It took them a few weeks to find the port and the boat to Europe. It was a small boat carrying 70 people. Without a whistle, the boat quietly left the port and started to voyage.

  After a few days on the Mediterranean Sea, the boat lost its direction. The food and water were getting less and less. Everyone gradually got into despair in the tremendous and deep sea. The only thing they could do was waiting for the current and wind to determine their destiny.
